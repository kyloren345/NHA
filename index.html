War Tactics

Most of the war tactics Napoleon employed were; his commandeering of his men and gaining respect from them, his surprise attacks and deception, speed by living off the land, decisive action, divide and conquer and innovative artillery tactics. These proved time and time again to aid him in victories over the duration of the Napoleonic Wars.

The First Coalition (1792 - 1797)

The First Coalition took place during 1792 and 1797 and this was the first time where Napoleon would lead an army into battle. As part the Continental System that Napoleon employed, France blocked off trade with Britain.

The Second Coalition (1798 - 1802)

During the Second Coalition Napoleon reformed the government system. He scaled back rights for certain groups - e.g women - but he was more fair towards other groups - e.g jews. He made the education system better and made opportunities based on education/talent not heritage/nobility. However, a portion of France's population demanded the return of the Church and revolted. After this revolution, Napoleon brought back the Church but restricted its power. "Overall, Napoleon's aim was to end the chaos of the revolution- and finally create a stable and prospering French Republic. And in that aim, he seemed to be largely successful."

The Third Coalition (1803 - 1806)

In the span of the Third Coaltion, Napoleon unveiled the reason as to why he was able to move so quickly and surprise his enemies. This was the creation of The Corp system, essentially what this system entailed was the use of living off the land gaining food and other resources. This meant that Napoleon didn't have to take his supply trains with him, meaning that he could travel much faster as everyone of his men is either on foot or on horse not having to pull the trains.

The Fourth Coalition (1806 - 1807)

The Fourth Coalition started off with the battle of Eylau which brought blizzard conditions many men from the Frech side either froze to death or were killed in combat due to the harsh weather conditions they hadn't expected. The Russian artillery severely outmatched the French and Napoleon himself was momentarily under risk of being captured. In the end, the French were brought to victory by a cavalry charge conducted by Napoleon's flamboyant cavalry commander, Murat. During the Battle of Friedland, Russian commander Bennigsen made a blunder, by positioning his men with their backs toward the river Aloe. This allowed Napoleon's men to fire artillery at the Russians and pin them against the river and gunfire.

The Fifth Coalition

In just four months Napoleon defeated Austria during the Fifth Coalition. The Austrians had been watching Napoleon and learning, and they had made some reformations to their tactics. In addition to this, Napoleon also had to rely on inexperienced conscripts after years of war. Around the same time, the Swedish people overthrew their old king leading to Bernadotte becoming King of Sweden.

The Sixth and Final Coalition

In the Sixth Coalition this is where Napoleon was finally defeated. It was between France and Russia mainly as Russia turned their backs on the Continental System and started trade with Britain again. This is where Napoleon declared war on Russia. He brought 650,000 men from all across his territory and assembled them with the intention of defeating Russia however, things didn't go as planned. Russia started retreating at the first contact with France and scorched the Earth as they went. This meant that Napoleon "couldn't live off the land, and if he couldn't live off the land, then he would need his supply trains, and if he needed his supply trains he couldn't travel as fast as he could" - Oversimplified. After a long period of chasing, Napoleon eventually made it to Moscow however winter conditions were on the way meaning it would get stupidly cold and Napoleon's men weren't wearing the correct winter clothing.
